Dosa

Description

A dosa is a thin, savoury crepe in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of ground white gram and rice. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar. Dosas are popular in South Asia as well as around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • ½ cup poha
  • ½ cup urad dal
  • 2 tablespoons chana dal
  • ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil or cooking spray

Steps

  1. Allow the rice and dal and other ingredients to soak for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  2. Blend the batter
  3. Place the batter in a large bowl, giving it room to expand, and then place it in a warm spot.
  4. Heat a well-seasoned cast iron griddle or a non-stick griddle over high heat.
  5. Pour the batter on the center of the griddle and, working quickly, move out in circles in a spiraling motion, spreading the batter with the bottom of the ladle.
  6. Sprinkle a few drops of oil around the dosa edges.
  7. Once the dosa edges look lacy and golden, and the top has dried out, loosen it gently from the griddle with a flat spatula. Fold the dosa in half and remove to a plate.
